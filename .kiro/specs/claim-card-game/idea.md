### **üî• Part 1: The Pitch - The Soul of the Game**

Imagine a game so simple, you can learn it in three minutes. But a game so brutal, it can end in thirty seconds. A game where a single card can be a triumph or a trap, and hesitation is the deadliest sin of all.

This is **CLAIM**.

It's a high-stakes duel of wits and nerve played with a standard deck of cards. You are dealt a mere three cards, and in that hand could be the key to a triumphant victory or a crippling, debt-ridden loss. The goal is to accumulate points by hoarding cards of a single suit. But to score, you must publicly **Declare** that suit, painting a target on your back for every other player to see.

The heart of the game is the ever-present threat of a **Showdown**. At any moment, any player can slam their cards down and force the game to an end. Those who were slow to commit, who were still building their hand or waiting for the perfect moment, are punished with severe, multiplied negative points.

A player lucky enough to start with a simple 2-3-4 run can end the game in a single, brutal "ambush" move. A player with only high cards like Ace and King can go all-in on a **"High Card Gambit"** and still win. This is a game where aggression is rewarded, patience is a monumental risk, and the clock is always ticking.

**CLAIM** is _simple as hell, brutal as the devil._

### **üß†‚öîÔ∏è Part 4: The Strategic Landscape - A Guide to Psychological Warfare**

**CLAIM** is more than a game of cards; it's a game of people. Master these concepts to master the game.

- **The High Card Gambit:** An all-in strategy of using high-value cards (A, K, Q) to win a Blitz Showdown, even without a run. It's a statement of pure aggression that can win the game before it begins.
- **The Ambush & The Rebuttal:** The core offensive/defensive dynamic. An aggressive player calls a Showdown with a weak hand, hoping no one can Rebut. A defensive player holds a 3-card run, knowing they can survive an ambush and turn the tables.
- **The Open Floor & The Art of the Block:** The rule that anyone can pick up a card, even to block another player, makes Declaring a suit a terrifying act of vulnerability. Watching an opponent pick up the Ace you need is a special kind of pain.
- **The Ticking Clock:** Your hand size is your potential penalty. The longer the game goes on, the more devastating a sudden Showdown becomes for the uncommitted. This forces action and prevents stagnation.
- **Patience vs. Aggression:** The central tension of CLAIM. Do you call a Showdown with a decent hand now, or wait for a perfect one and risk someone else ending the game first?

#### **Advanced Tactics & Player Dynamics**

- **The "Kingmaker" Scenario:** Imagine a 4-player game where Player A has an unbeatable hand. Player D, with no chance of winning, can now decide who loses the _most_. By strategically picking up or declining cards, Player D can sabotage one opponent to help another, effectively choosing second place. This isn't a flaw; it's a feature of high-stakes multiplayer games and a core part of the psychological warfare.

### **üìñ Part 5: The Lexicon - The Language of CLAIM**

- **Declare:** The act of publicly committing to a suit.
- **Showdown:** The act of ending the round to force scoring.
- **Blitz Showdown:** A Showdown called on the very first turn of the round (The Ambush).
- **Rebuttal:** A defensive move where an undeclared player reveals a 3-card run to contest a Showdown.
- **Hoarder's Multiplier:** The scoring system where the number of declared suit cards acts as a multiplier.
- **The Open Floor:** The principle that any revealed card is available to any player, enabling blocking strategies.
- **The Final Reckoning:** The final round of action that occurs when the draw deck is empty.
- **High Card Gambit:** An all-in strategy of using high-value cards to win a Blitz Showdown.
- **Clean Sweep Bonus:** A 50-point bonus for having a hand with only your declared suit cards.
- **Long Run Bonus:** A 25-point bonus for each run of 4+ consecutive cards.
